This work examines whether the tracheal respiratory system of the beetle *Platynus decentis* follows classical Murray’s law. Using synchrotron x-ray microtomography and three-dimensional reconstruction, tracheal branching geometries were quantified across the insect respiratory network. The observed branching exponent deviates from the classical cubic scaling prediction, suggesting that insect tracheal systems are governed by mixed advective–diffusive transport mechanisms rather than purely viscous optimization. The study also evaluates a modified Murray’s law framework incorporating hydrodynamic slip, diffusion, and alternative metabolic scaling relevant to insect respiration and respiratory biomechanics.
