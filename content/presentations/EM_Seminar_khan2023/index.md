abstract: |
  Insects breathe using a network of tracheal tubes that bring respiratory gases directly to the tissues without using an intermediate oxygen carrier like blood. This mechanism inspires advancements in microfluidic technology; however, our understanding of the fundamental principles of insect respiration is limited. In this study, we consider the effect of irregularities in the tracheal wall, which contains local, periodic, ’bump’-like, microscale thickenings called taenidia. We carried out 3D finitevolume simulations of air flow in idealized tracheal models and analyzed the effects of the geometric parameters of the taenidia. We found that the presence of taenidia can accelerate peak flow, enhancing longitudinal dispersion. We further compared the flow in the idealized trachea to the flow through an anatomically accurate tracheal model from the darkling beetle Zophobas morio and found that small differences from the idealized geometries could lead to significant differences in flow patterns.
